PROCESS

The Data

Having worked in clinical trials, I am in general very interested in health care so I wanted to do some analysis on how different diseases impact different countries. I found this very interesting dataset that shows information about 15 diseases in 31 European countries as well as the European Union as a whole. The dataset shows what % of females, males as well as the total population has been affected by each condition.

Data Analysis

I created multiple visualizations exploring the data and here I am showing the total population affected by each disease in each country. I thought that this visualization was very beautiful but completely unreadable.

I then created a bar graph showing how each condition affects the total population of the European Union countries only. While this is very interesting it was not what I wanted to show as the information is too little.

Next I explored the possibility of presenting the data on a map. I merged the dataset with a geojson file and selected a subset of one condition only (Arthrosis). I really liked that but then one could only see the data for either the total population or for females or for males separately.

Finally, I decided to visualize the data as a grouped bar chart taking Bulgaria as an example. This allows to show any differences, influenced by the gender of the population, clearly showing that some conditions are more prevalent in females while others in males.

After comparing this visualization type for a few countries, I thought it is the most appropriate way to visualize the data as it allowed for the most thorough analysis. I recreated the visualization in D3.js and added a dropdown to allow users to select which country they are interested in.

Data Preparation

I struggled a bit with formatting the data in JavaScript and exporting them and finally discovered that I needed to pivot the Pandas dataframe and make the health conditions the columns.
