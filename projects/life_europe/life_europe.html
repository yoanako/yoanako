PROCESS

The Data

As I was looking at different datasets, I was searching for a dataset that can be represented as a map since I wanted to learn how to program geographical data in D3. I found this dataset that contains interesting statistics for 32 European countries.

Data Analysis

I first made several bar graphs to help me select the statistics I would be working with. The original dataset contained 36 indicators and I knew this would be too much to show. Some of the indicators immediately jumped out at me as there were very clear outliers and correlations.

I was then ready to start mapping the data with GeoPandas so I merged the dataset with a geojson file. Once again I made maps of all the indicators and confirmed the ones I wanted to include in the D3 visualization.

Here are some of the maps from GeoPandas that I found very interesting and some of which I later translated into D3.js. An example of a finding that was very interesting is that the countries that have higher average hours worked were also the countries that have lowest median income. These were also the countries that had the lowest life expectancy.

What is next?

In general I like the mapping approach for this data but I would like to come up with a way to show multiple indicators at the same time. I am not sure how to do it successfully on a map yet but it remains a project I am very interested in expanding.
